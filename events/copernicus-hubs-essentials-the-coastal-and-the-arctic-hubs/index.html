Copernicus Hubs Essentials - The Coastal and the Arctic hubs

12 September 2025 – 13 September 2025

Noveltis, Copernicus Marine Service, Mercator Ocean International Online

The Copernicus Marine Service is hosting a 2-part webinar series for beginners, designed to engage Earth Observation communities worldwide. This series is perfect for first-time users looking to explore, access, and use Coastal and Arctic Copernicus data with confidence. By the end, participants will have a clear understanding of all availableHubsresources and how to apply them to their work. This 2-part webinar series will follow this agenda: Session #1 - Copernicus Coastal Hub (12 Sept) Get introduced to the platform: data catalogue, Viewer and real-world applications showcasing the use of data in coastal regions. Session #2 - Copernicus Arctic Hub (19 Sept) Get introduced to the platform: data catalogue, Viewer and real-world applications showcasing the use of arctic data. Each session will  featureguest speakersfrom the Earth Observation community, who will share their experiences and showcase practical applications of data available on the Copernicus Hubs.

Space for Neurodiversity

Space for Neurodiversity is an inclusive initiative advancing understanding, visibility, and meaningful participation of neurodivergent people across the space sector. We bring together lived experience, research, and industry collaboration to shape more inclusive cultures, policies, and workplaces in space and adjacent industries.
